Interested in eating better, but find it difficult to do a regular basis? Today, we are simply too busy to think about what to eat, let alone take time to plan and prepare good wholesome home-cooked meals. As a result, we end up eating whatever is quick and convenient, even though we know it may not be the best choice. Having successfully navigated the transition from convenience eating to clean eating myself, I believe I have an excellent handle on what it takes to be successful in managing meals. And, I want to build a software application that will make it easier to manage meals week-in and week-out. The goal of this project is to raise funds that will allow me to build the system. What's in it for you? Contributors will have two rewards: 1) Free license to the final program, and 2) creative input.

The basic premise is as follows: to be successful, you need to have a plan. The software gives you a platform to plan your meals over a time period, say a week. You select your meals from a pallet of items that you know how to prepare or can purchase at your favorite store. As you build your mealplan, the system will keep track of the daily nutrition profile. So, you can choose foods that fit your desired nutrition profile in realtime. You decide what nutrients you want to track. If you don't know, the system will default to the USDA recommendations for daily nutrition requirements. You can change them at anytime. Once you are satisfied with your meal plan, use your smart phone to follow the plan and track your progress. You can make on-the-spot updates, should circumstances dictate a change. And, the program will also track the items that you need to purchase at the grocery store for home prepared meals. For example, it's smart enough to figure out how much chicken you need when you are having chicken in several meals in the plan. After all, you should only have to make one trip to the grocery store. But, wait! It's also smart enough to give you advance notice when you need to have that chicken clean for use in a recipe later in the week. All of the meal planning/organizing activities are predetermined for once you enter the recipe.
